## Loop 24 Proposals

### P1: Progressive Tense Overuse — `grammar.js` ⭐ HIGH ROI
- **Rationale:** Chinese L1 learners overuse progressive ("is studying" instead of "studies") — Xu & Ellis (2020) confirms 2x native frequency in TOEFL essays
- **Implementation:** Count progressive forms per 100 words; >4/100w → penalty signal in grammar.js
- **Pattern:** `/\b(is|are|was|were|am)\s+\w+ing\b/gi` — rate-based, ≥5 occurrences → flag
- **FP risk:** LOW — native essays rarely exceed 3-4 per 100w in academic writing
- **Est. QWK gain:** +0.02–0.04

### P2: Sentence Length Variance — `style.js` ⭐
- **Rationale:** Score-5 essays show higher sentence length variance (SD ~8-12) vs Score-3 (SD ~3-5) — Crossley et al. (2016) TAALES
- **Implementation:** Compute mean/variance of sentence lengths; low variance (<5 SD) → -0.02
- **FP risk:** LOW — purely statistical, no lexical FP
- **Est. QWK gain:** +0.02–0.05

### P3: Comparative "than" Errors — `grammar.js`
- **Rationale:** Chinese uses "比" for comparison; learners confuse "more...then" → "more...than"
- **Pattern:** `/\b(more|less|better|worse|higher|lower|greater)\s+\w+\s+then\b/gi`
- **FP risk:** VERY LOW — "more X then Y" is almost always an error
- **Est. QWK gain:** +0.01–0.03

### P4: Paragraph Length Balance — `organization.js`
- **Rationale:** Score-5 essays have more balanced paragraph lengths (Ferris 2014 corpus); extreme imbalance (1 para 3x another) signals underdevelopment
- **Implementation:** Compare paragraph char counts; ratio >3:1 → -0.02 penalty
- **FP risk:** LOW for emails (structure varies), MEDIUM for discussions
- **Est. QWK gain:** +0.01–0.03
