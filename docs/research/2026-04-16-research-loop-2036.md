## L67A: Conjunctive Adverb Comma-Splice

### Error Pattern

Chinese EFL learners write:

> "The data was clear, **therefore** we accepted the conclusion."  
> "The plan was expensive, **moreover** it was inefficient."

Correct form requires semicolon before the adverb:

> "The data was clear; **therefore**, we accepted the conclusion."

### Validation Findings

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|-----------|
| Frequency in CLEC | Comma splices with adverbial conjunctions are a documented CLEC error category. However-specific rate not separately reported, but combined incidence meaningful. |
| False positive risk | **Very low** — rule is unambiguous in formal academic writing. No legitimate exception for `, however [clause]` form. |
| FP guards needed | Require subject/article after adverb (blocks parenthetical "we should, however, try" and degree "however difficult it may be"). |

### Implementation

Pattern: `,\s+(however|moreover|therefore|furthermore|consequently|nevertheless|nonetheless|thus|hence)\s+(I\b|he\b|she\b|they\b|we\b|it\b|this\b|that\b|the\b|a\b|an\b|[A-Z][a-z])`
