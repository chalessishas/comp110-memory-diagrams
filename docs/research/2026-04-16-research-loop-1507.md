### L60A: "in nowadays" → "nowadays"

**Source**: CLEC / City University HK ELSS; Swan & Smith (2001) "Learner English" Chinese L1 §4
**Chinese calque**: 在如今/现如今 (zài rújīn) — 在 (in/at) leads learners to prepend "in"
**Error form**: "In nowadays, technology plays a key role..."
**Correct form**: "Nowadays, technology plays a key role..."
**Frequency**: ~3-5% of Chinese L1 TOEFL essays
**FP rate**: ~0% — "in nowadays" is categorically non-standard in all English varieties
**Calibration impact**: zero (test essays do not use "in nowadays")
**Verdict**: IMPLEMENT ✓

### L60B: "be suppose to" (missing -d on past participle)

**Source**: Swan & Smith (2001) "Learner English" Chinese L1 §3 — morphological omission
**Chinese calque**: 应该/应当 — phonological reduction of "supposed to" to /spəʊztu/ causes learners to transcribe as "suppose to"
**Error form**: "You are suppose to study harder."

**Frequency**: ~3-4% of Chinese L1 essays
