### L62A: "be base on" → "be based on"
- **Type**: Morphology error (missing -d past participle)
- **Regex**: `/(am|is|are|was|were|be|been)\s+base\s+on\b/i`
- **Source**: Swan & Smith (2001) Chinese L1 §3; CLEC ~5-7% frequency
- **Mechanism**: Chinese 基于 (jī yú) lacks participial -d morphology; learners transcribe passive "is based on" as "is base on" by phonological transfer
- **FP rate**: ~0% — "be base on" is categorically non-standard; "is/are based on" (correct) doesn't match
- **Example**: "The theory is base on three core principles." → "is based on"

### L62B: "be worth of" → "worth [gerund]" / "worthy of"
- **Type**: Collocation error (conflation of two constructions)
- **Regex**: `/\b(?:is|are|was|were|be|been|seem[s]?|looked?)\s+worth\s+of\b/i`
- **Source**: Gu et al. (2013) Chinese EFL collocation atlas; CLEC ~3-5% frequency
- **Mechanism**: Chinese 值得做 (zhí de zuò) maps to both "worth doing" AND "worthy of doing" in English; learners add "of" after "worth" by analogy with "worthy of"
- **FP guard**: Copula guard excludes "X worth of Y" quantity expressions (no "be" before "worth of" in those)
- **FP rate**: ~0% with copula guard
- **Example**: "It is worth of studying technology." → "worth studying" (no "of")

### L62C: "make effort to" → "make an effort to"
- **Type**: Article error (missing indefinite article "an")
- **Regex**: `/\bmake(?:s|d|ing)?\s+effort\s+to\b/i`
- **Source**: Hawkins & Buttery (2010) Chinese L1 article omission; CLEC ~5% frequency
- **Mechanism**: Chinese 努力 (nǔ lì) is an uncountable noun-verb with no article equivalent; learners omit "an" before "effort" by zero-article transfer
- **FP guard**: Direct adjacency regex excludes "make every/great/more effort to" — only bare "make effort to" triggers
- **FP rate**: ~0%
- **Example**: "Students should make effort to practice daily." → "make an effort to"
