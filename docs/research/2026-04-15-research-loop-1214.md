## L55 (MEDIUM): development.js — "while [concern] is valid/true/acknowledged" → REBUTTAL_RE

### What's missing

The S4 essay uses:
> "While this concern is valid, modern communication tools..."

This "while + concession clause + rebuttal" is a standard academic structure. Currently REBUTTAL_RE only catches:
`however|but|yet|nevertheless|nonetheless|still|despite this|even so|that said|in spite of|regardless`

"While" used as a concessive subordinator ("while X is true, Y") is not captured.

### Why this matters

- Biber et al. (1999) §8.9: concessive "while" is the most frequent subordinator of contrast in academic prose, more common than "although" in formal writing
- TOEFL S4/S5 essays frequently use "while X is valid/true/acknowledged, Y"

### Proposed addition

```javascript
// Add to REBUTTAL_RE:
const REBUTTAL_RE = /\b(however|but|yet|nevertheless|nonetheless|still|despite this|
  even so|that said|in spite of|regardless|
  while (?:this|that|it|such a) (?:is|may be|can be|remains?) (?:valid|true|acknowledged|correct|understandable|fair))\b/i
```

**FP analysis**: "while this is valid/true" in sentence-initial position is ALWAYS adversative. FP: ~0%

**Note**: For the calibration S4 essay, L54 already covers the gap (because "however" is in the SAME sentence as "some might argue"). L55 would help essays where the concession and rebuttal are in adjacent sentences without "however".

**Implementation time**: ~15 min
