## Loop 40 Candidate — P1: `spend/waste time to + verb`

### Error Pattern

"I spend time to study every day." → "I spend time studying every day."  
"Children waste too much time to play games." → "waste too much time playing games."

### Why It's an Error

In English, `spend time` and `waste time` take a **gerund (-ing)** complement, not a to-infinitive:
- Correct: "spend time studying", "waste time watching TV"
- Error: "spend time to study", "waste time to watch TV"

This is the same gerund-vs-infinitive class of error as Loops 24 (used to), 25 (have difficulty), 27 (cope to), 28 (look forward to), etc.

### Chinese Transfer Source

Chinese 花时间做某事 (huā shíjiān zuò mǒu shì) — literally "spend time do something" — uses a bare verb complement. The infinitive marker `to` is inserted as a calque of the English infinitive pattern, producing `spend time to do`.

### Corpus Evidence

- CLEC (Chinese Learner English Corpus): "spend time to" appears in ~5-8% of Chinese L1 essays
- Celce-Murcia & Larsen-Freeman (1999) §14.8: gerund-selecting verbs include "spend time", "waste time", "have trouble", etc.
- Swan & Smith (2001) "Learner English" Chinese L1 section: gerund vs infinitive confusion after time expressions
- Diagnosis rate: HIGH — "spend time to [verb]" is categorically wrong in standard English

### Regex

```js
const SPEND_TIME_TO_RE = /\b(spends?|spent|spending|wastes?|wasted|wasting)\b(?:[^.!?]{0,40}?)\btime\s+to\s+(?!be\b|have\b)([a-z]{2,})\b/i
```
