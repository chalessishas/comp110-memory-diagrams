## Remaining High-Value Proposals (from prior research)

### From 0650 Research Doc (still unimplemented):

**P3: "The reason is because" → "the reason is that"**
- Hinkel (2002): "the reason is because" is a prescriptively condemned redundant structure
- Error: "The reason is because technology is useful" → correct: "The reason is that..."
- Regex: `/\bthe\s+reason\s+(?:is|was)\s+because\b/i` — near ~0% FP
- Est. freq: ~8% Chinese L1 essays

**P4: "As far as I concern" → "As far as I'm concerned"**  
- Swan & Smith (2001): frozen idiom mislearning — learners omit "am" from the phrase
- Regex: `/\bas\s+far\s+as\s+(?:I|we)\s+concern\b/i` — ~0% FP (only wrong form triggers)
- Est. freq: ~5%

**P5: Sentence-initial "besides" overuse (style penalty)**
- Tseng (2015): 4× native rate; sentence-initial "besides" is informal in academic register
- Register penalty, not a grammar error

## New WebSearch Findings for Loop 28

Based on synthesis of CLEC corpus studies (checked this session):

### Proposal A: "Suggest/recommend/insist + base verb" (subjunctive omission)
- Error: "I suggest you study harder" is actually CORRECT American English (mandative subjunctive)
- Chinese learners often write "I suggest you to study" (spurious "to") — ALREADY COVERED by GERUND patterns
- **SKIP** — already handled

### Proposal B: "Make contribution/effect/impact to/on" misuse
- Chinese 对...有贡献 (have contribution to) → "make contribution to society" vs correct "contribute to society" or "make a contribution to society"
- COLLOCATION_ERRORS probably doesn't cover "make contribution" (missing article) or "have contribution"
- Check: `/\bhave\s+(?:a\s+)?(?:great|big|positive|significant|important)?\s*contribution\s+(?:to|on|for)\b/i`
- But "make a contribution to" IS correct — only "have contribution" and "make contribution" (no article) are errors
- FP risk: MEDIUM — careful regex needed

### Proposal C: "Pay attention to + gerund" vs infinitive
- "Pay attention to learn" → "pay attention to learning" (same "used to" gerund trap)
- Regex: `/\bpay\s+attention\s+to\s+(?!the\b|this\b|that\b|my\b|your\b|a\b|an\b)([a-z]{3,})\b(?<!ing)\b/i`
- But this is complex and medium FP

### Proposal D: "According to + finite clause" (already noted)
- "According to the research shows that" → "According to the research, it shows that" or "Research shows that"
- Already have "according to + pronoun" — extend to "according to + [NP] + verb"
