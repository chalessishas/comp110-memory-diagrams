# Local Community Garden Opens in Riverside Park

A new community garden has opened in Riverside Park. The garden opened last Saturday and is available to all residents of the area. City officials and community members attended the opening ceremony.

The garden is located on the east side of Riverside Park, where an unused parking lot used to be. The project was funded by a $45,000 grant from the State Department of Agriculture and additional donations from local businesses. Planning for the garden began about eighteen months ago when several residents approached the city council with the idea.

Mayor Linda Chen spoke at the opening. "This garden represents what our community can accomplish when we work together," she said. "We hope it will bring people closer to nature and to each other." About seventy people attended the event, which included refreshments provided by a local bakery.

The garden has forty individual plots that residents can rent for $25 per season. Each plot is four feet by eight feet. There is also a shared area where volunteers will grow produce that will be donated to the local food bank. Garden coordinator Tom Reeves said that about thirty of the forty plots have already been claimed.

Residents who want a plot can sign up at the Parks and Recreation office or on the city website. The growing season runs from April through October. The garden provides access to water and basic tools, but gardeners are expected to bring their own seeds and plants.

Several other cities in the region have started similar community garden programs in recent years. Studies suggest that community gardens can improve neighborhood satisfaction and increase property values, though results vary depending on the location.

The garden will be open daily from 7 a.m. to 8 p.m. More information is available on the city website.
