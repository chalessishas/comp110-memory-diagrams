# Why Public Libraries Matters More Than People Think

Most people thinks of libraries as outdated places where old books collect dust but this view are completely wrong. Public libraries is one of the most important institutions in a democratic society and there decline would effect vulnerable communities the hardest. The argument I wants to make is simple, libraries does far more than lend books they serve as equalizers in an unequal world.

First consider the digital divide. About 24 million Americans doesn't have reliable internet access at home according to the FCC. For these people the library are often the only place they can goes to use a computer, libraries provides free wifi, printing services and access to databases that would otherwise costs hundreds of dollars per year. When a single mother need to file her taxes online or a veteran need to apply for benefits digitally the library is where they goes. Without this access they is effectively locked out of modern civic life, this is not a small problem.

Second libraries serves as community mental health resources even though most people don't realizes it. Librarians across the country has reported that they regularly encounters patrons experiencing homelessness, addiction and mental health crisis. Many library systems has responded by hiring social workers, the San Francisco Public Library were one of the first to do this back in 2009 and the results was striking. Referrals to shelters and treatment programs increased by 300 percent in the first year.

The organizational capacity of libraries also make them uniquely positioned for disaster response. During Hurricane Harvey Houston's libraries becomes emergency shelters and distribution centers almost overnight. This happen because libraries already has the trust of there communities, the physical space and the trained staff who know how to manage information and people.

Critics argues that the internet have made libraries obsolete but this argument fundamentally misunderstand what libraries does. A search engine don't help you evaluate sources, it don't connect you with social services and it certainly don't provide a warm safe space on a cold night. Libraries is not about books anymore, they is about equity. Cutting there funding don't save money it just shift costs onto emergency rooms homeless shelters and an increasingly uninformed public.
