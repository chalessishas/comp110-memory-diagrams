## The Science Behind Detection

AI-generated text has a statistical fingerprint. Large language models like GPT-4, Claude, and Gemini generate text by predicting the next most likely token. This creates patterns that are invisible to readers but measurable by math.

### Signal 1: Perplexity

Perplexity measures how "surprised" a language model is by the text. If the model finds the text very predictable (low perplexity), it was likely generated by a similar model.

- **AI text**: Perplexity 3-8 (very predictable)
- **Human text**: Perplexity 20-50 (surprising, unpredictable)

### Signal 2: GLTR Token Rank Distribution

For each token, we check: was this word in the model's top 10 predictions? Top 100? Top 1000? AI text uses top-10 tokens over 90% of the time. Human text is far more varied.

### Signal 3: Entropy

Entropy measures uncertainty at each token position. AI text has low entropy. Human text creates more uncertainty.

### Signal 4: Burstiness

Human writing is "bursty" — sentence lengths vary dramatically. AI writing is remarkably uniform.

### Signal 5: Vocabulary Diversity

We measure Type-Token Ratio and hapax legomena. Humans use more unique words.

## Why 5 Signals, Not 1?

Any single signal can be fooled. But defeating all 5 simultaneously is extremely difficult. We show our work so you can judge for yourself.
